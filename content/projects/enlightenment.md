Enligtenment is a large scale installation that merges astrophysical research, neuroscience, and spiritual symbolism into spatial experience. The project originated from the study of black hole physics, particularly the structure of the event horizon, accretion disk, singularity, and the bending of light thorugh gravitational lensing. The initial concept proposed a black metal sphere, cold and opaque, enclosing a radiant core of heat and light: a physical analogy to a black hole's invisible interior.

During the development of the work, the discovery of living with aphantasia, an inability to generate mental images, shifted the conceptual axis of the project. The black sphere ceased to function solely as a cosmological model and began to represent the mind itself. The light within came to symbolize conceptual thought, ideas emerging not as images but as structures, non-visual cognition.

The installatio evolved into an architectural passage. The viewer enters a room containing a monumental sphere (approximately 250 cm in diametry), constructed from 184 segmented panels arranged in calibrated rows to maintain curvature. The exterior remains matte and sealed, preventing any internal light from leaking outward. A discrete opening allows entry to the spherical chamber.

Inside, a vertical column of warm light descends from above, made visible through atmospheric haze. The space compresses perception: the curved interior walls eliminate corners and orientation, producing a sense of enclosure analogues to the skull. The central light becomes both singularity and origin point, a metaphorical locus of thought. A cushion placed beneath the light introduces the possibility of stillness and meditation, transforming the interior into a contemplative chamber rather than a purely sculptural volume.

Further research into brain anatomy led to the symbolic integration of the pineal gland, historically associated with immagination, perception, and the so called "third eye". While biologically understood as an endocrine gland regulating circadian rhythms, it has accumulated layered meanings across philosophical and esoteric traditions, from René Descartes'proposal of it as the seat of the soul to its identification in various spiritual systems as a gateway to higher perceptions. This convergence of scientific fact and symbolic projection reinforces the work's dual structure: empirical research interwined with metaphysical speculation.

Formally the piece stages a progression: from the external world (the gallery space), into the dark sphere (the mind), and finally toward the illuminated core (conceptual emergence of enlightenment). What cannot be seen from outside becomes fully experienced from within.

Enlightenment thus operates on parallel registers. Cosmologically it references the physics of black holes and the limits of visibility. Cognitively, it reflects a mode of thinking detached from imagery. Spiritually, it evokes initiation and ascent. Rather than offering a spectacle, the installation constructs a controlled environment in which perception, orientation, and self-awareness are recalibrated through spatial immersion.
