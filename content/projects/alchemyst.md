AlchemYst in Progress is a living installation that situates alchemical symbolism within the concrete processes of botanical life. At its center stands a compact sphere of soil, enclosed within a transparent, spherical glass vase. This vessel operates both as container and lens: it isolates the soil mass from its sourroundings while making its internal processes visible. The sphere becomes a contemporary Prima Materia, dense, undifferentiated ground held in suspension, observed rather than dispersed.

From this contained earth rise three plants, each aligned with a principal stage of the alchemical Magnum Opus. Their growth introduces vertical movement against the closed curvature of the glass, creating a tension between containment and expansion.

The soil sphere functions simultaniously as atructural support and conceptual anchor. Whithin it, decomposition and germination unfold in parallel, decay serving as precondition to renewal. The glass enclosure intensifies this reading: it suggests laboratory apparatus, preservation chamber, or alchemical flask, positioning the work between scientific observation and symbolic ritual. The viewer encounters not only plants, but a microcosm, an ecosystem framed, circumscribed, and deliberate.

By grounding alchemical language in living matter the sculpture reframes spiritual trasmutation as a material and ecological process. Transformation appears neither abstract nor instantaneous; it's gradual, metabolic, and contingent on environment. The transparent sphere makes this explicit: change is visible yet never fully controllable.

In this configuration, AlchemYst in Progress propose that illumination does not trascend matter but emerges from within it, through compression, decay, and sustained attention.
