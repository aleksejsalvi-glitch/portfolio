MAGNUM OPUS

SEPTEMBER 23, 2025

×

Magnum Opus is an artistic and experimental project that merges ceramic research with alchemical symbolism. The work consists of a vertical cabinet divided into four layers, each representing one of the traditional stages of alchemy: Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, and Rubedo. Whithin each level are displayed materials collected and developed during the research process: samples of raw minerals, high-temperature glaze tests, and ceramic objects. The material used include chrysocolla, aquamarine, diopside, obsidian, and lapis lazuli, all integrated into glaze recipes developed from natural raw materials.

The structure of the cabinet is built from metal and glass, and conceived as a visual and tactile archive of the transformation of matter. The project documents the passage from raw materials to ceramic form, exploring the reactions of natural materials in the kiln and the way they transform in surface, color, and texture.

Magnum Opus is a process of transformation, purification, and realization, both spiritual and physical. A work that is born from the earth and, through fire, manifest itself in its highest expression. Following the four main stages of alchemy: Nigredo, Albedo, Citrinitas, and Rubedo, the work unfolds as an initiatory journey in wigh each shelf of the cabinet represents a symbolic and material stage of change. Minerals become powder. Powder, glazes. Mistakes become revelations. Nothing is discarded. Everything is part of the research. Fire, as in alchemy, does not destroy but transform.

Magnum Opus does not seek to arrive at a final formula, but to honor the act of transformation itself. The cabinet stands as a container of process, a ritual object, a record of trasmutation, and a quiet homage to the invisible layers of change.
